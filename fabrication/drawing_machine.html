The Ephemeral Drawing Machine

This was a group project for PS70: Introduction to Digital Fabrication, a class about building physical things with digital tools. We built a drawing machine that dispenses ink onto water, creating shapes that briefly appear on the surface before dissolving into marbled patterns.

Project overview

Our assignment was to build a drawing machine that could render user-input shapes and reliably return to a consistent origin. We began with a starter kit of hardware and code, then adapted both to create something more like an art installation.

During brainstorming, our group considered a few different ideas, including Spirograph-like mechanisms. We eventually drew inspiration from a water-marbling project and decided to build a machine that would draw directly onto the surface of water.

How it worked

The machine used two stepper motors to control the x-axis and y-axis movement of the pen, and a servo motor to control the z-axis motion. The pen could move to a starting coordinate, lower toward the water surface, and draw shapes based on user input.

Instead of drawing on paper, the machine dispensed ink onto water. This created two outputs: the plotted shape made by the machine, and the secondary marbled pattern formed when paper was pressed onto the inked water.

My role

I worked mainly on the coding and wiring with Yuhan. This involved modifying the starter code, working with the websocket interface, and helping debug the motion of the x-axis, y-axis, and z-axis.

The project involved a lot of troubleshooting. Some issues came from belt slippage, loose wires, stepper motor behavior, servo control, and getting the ink pen to dispense consistently. After a lot of testing and help from the teaching team, we were able to get the machine working.
